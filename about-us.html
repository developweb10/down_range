About Down Range Dynamic Solutions

Our goal, as a business, is to provide both new and experienced shooters with high quality products that best suits the customer's situational requirements. We strive for the highest quality control standards. To meet those standards, each rifle is built by one person from start to finish, and each firearm sold is test fired with the target and spent ammunition casings included in each box, unless the customer specifies otherwise. It’s our goal for us to correct any potential issues, rather than for our customers to have the inconvenience of returning products, requiring additional cost and time before the firearm can be enjoyed.

We have combined personal firearm experience of over 40 years. We strive to also provide our customers with the best experience in the firearm industry. Excellent customer service and quality products are our main goals. Charles G. Spiars is CEO, and an avid firearm enthusiast of over a decade. He thoroughly enjoys target shooting, hunting and fishing various game, building custom rifles, and critiquing various firearms and ammunitions with a mind toward product improvement. Charles ("Trey") has over 12 years experience in the oil field industry in Mississippi, and lately North Dakota, being a welder/fabricator, machinist, driver, heavy equipment operator, and tow boat deck hand. He comes from a hardworking background, with a strong sense of integrity and morality. He enjoys introducing new and future firearm enthusiasts to the joys of firearms, and finding their preferences. He believes firearm safety is paramount, and firearm care and maintenance is a must.

Thomas L. Helms is COO, and also a firearm enthusiast with over three decades of firearm experience. He enjoys target shooting, hunting and fishing various game, teaching new hunters and anglers the joys of the sports, building custom rifles, and critiquing various firearms and ammunitions also with a mind toward improving current products. Thomas ("Tommy") has over 36 years experience in various fields of employment in Mississippi. He has worked as a butcher for both resident livestock and game, both full and part time, for 35 years, as well as in various positions within the agricultural industry and Mississippi Department of Transportation. His heart lies with scouting, guiding, and tracking for youth disability hunts, and believes firearm safety being learned and practiced at the very beginning of firearm experience is most important.
